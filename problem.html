The issue

The problem

According to the World Bank, in 2025, approximately 81% of the population of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) lives in poverty despite the country possessing some of the world's largest reserves of cobalt, copper, gold, and coltan. Although these resources could significantly improve living standards, corruption, illegal mining, and weak governance prevent citizens from benefiting. These challenges have contributed to widespread poverty, social conflict, and limited access to essential services such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure. Understanding how to manage the DRC's natural resources effectively is critical for promoting sustainable development. How can transparent and effective management of natural resources in the DRC ensure that resource wealth improves citizens' living standards and promotes sustainable economic growth by 2035?

Root cause

5 Whys: why poverty persists

1

Why does poverty persist despite resources? Resources aren't used for the people.

2

Why don't resources improve living standards? Wealth is captured before it reaches citizens.

3

Why is wealth captured? Corruption, weak governance, and illegal mining.

4

Why is governance too weak? Conflict and instability.

5

Why do corruption and weak governance persist? Weak institutions, poor accountability, political instability.

Voices

Voices from our research

Trust deficit

Every interviewee reported low-to-no trust in resource-revenue management and questioned whether public money reached intended services.

Conflict and displacement

Mining is directly linked to conflict and displacement, especially where land rights and security remain unstable.

Living conditions

Community quality of life was rated “Very Poor,” with limited access to schools, health services, and safe transport.

Leadership demand

Citizens want accountable, inclusive leadership and fair revenue distribution across education, healthcare, and infrastructure.

Rural exclusion

Many residents said they felt unheard by decision-makers and disconnected from public reporting systems.

Hope for change

People still believe visible, verifiable public systems could rebuild trust and improve everyday life.

Context

PESTLE summary

Political

Transparency International rankings show weak public trust and limited accountability in resource governance.

Economic

Central bank rates at 25% and inflation at 7.5% have intensified household pressure and reduced economic resilience.

Social

Conflict and displacement are driving social fragmentation, while communities struggle to access training and services.

Technological

Limited rural ICT access makes digital-only solutions difficult to scale without offline-first design.

Legal

The 2018 Mining Code set royalties at 10%, but weak enforcement leaves revenue management vulnerable to abuse.

Environmental

Mining runoff continues to threaten the Congo River basin and the health of surrounding communities.
